# How 3.1 + 3.2 Make This App Different from a Commercial AI SaaS (e.g., ChatGPT)

## Executive summary
This app is not just a general chatbot. With Feature 3.1 and 3.2, it behaves like a **domain-governed ADU regulation assistant** designed for planning workflows, where freshness, traceability, and regulatory alignment matter more than broad conversational ability.

## What is different

### 1) Source-governed knowledge, not generic internet memory
- **3.1 Dynamic Regulation Tracking** continuously syncs ADU sources (state bills and handbook content) into a managed knowledge base.
- Responses are grounded in the app’s current regulation dataset, instead of relying mainly on a model’s general pretraining.

### 2) Built for regulation change, not static answers
- The system is designed around law updates: ingest, detect changes, and refresh indexed knowledge.
- This reduces the risk of stale guidance when ADU rules change.

### 3) Version-aware responses for decision confidence
- 3.1 explicitly tracks versions and update timing, enabling users to understand *which rule state* an answer reflects.
- That version context is critical for planning, permitting, and internal review.

### 4) ADU-specific query handling (3.2)
- The app is tuned for recurring planning questions (height, setbacks, design constraints, compliance checks).
- Answers are shaped for this domain, rather than broad open-domain Q&A.

### 5) Operational fit for planning teams
- Sync status, source links, and regulation-focused UI create a workflow tool, not just a chat interface.
- The result is better continuity for staff use cases where consistency and accountability are required.

## Practical implication
For consumer tasks, a commercial AI SaaS is usually enough. For ADU planning operations, this app provides stronger **freshness control, domain alignment, and response accountability** by combining 3.1 tracking with 3.2 targeted handling.
